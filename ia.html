Rules

The Go game was born in China there is several thousand years. It takes place in Japan for 1200 years, but it spread only recently in west. The purpose of the game is the constitution of territories by using a material of the simplest: a board, called goban, on which is drawn a grid and pawns, called stones, which we put on the intersections of this grid alternately.

Two intersections are said neighbours when they are on the same line and without other intersection between them.

Two stones are nearby if they occupy nearby intersections.

A chain is a set of one or several stones of even color nearby step by step.

The liberties of a chain are the nearby idle intersections of stones of this chain(channel).

A territory is a set of one or several nearby idle intersections step by step, bounded by stones of the same color.

When a player deletes the last freedom of one opposite chain, it captures it by removing from the goban stones of this chain. Furthermore, by laying a stone, a player does not have to build a chain without freedom, unless by this blow he captures an opposite chain.
When a chain does not have more than a freedom, we say that it is atari.

Of the rule of capture ensues the notion of life and death : dead stones are stones which we are sure to be able to capture without losing there besides, whereas alive stones are stones which we cannot hope any more to capture.

According to the rule of capture, White can play in 'A' and take Black.

The part stops when both players pass in succession. We count then points. Every intersection of the territory of a player brings back him a point, as well as each of its still present stones on the goban. The winner is the one who has most points.
